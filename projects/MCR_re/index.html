Manhattan Crash Report

According to the government's report, there are about 6,000,000 traffic accidents across the United States every year, and 1/30 of them happens in New York City. In 2018, there were 228,047 motor vehicle accidents in all of New York City. This includes collisions involving all motorists, bicyclists, and pedestrians. That breaks down to about 20,000 car accidents every month and over 600 every day in NYC.

For residents live in New York, it is particularly important to know the facts of New York's traffic situation. This report will narrow the scope down to the traffic accidents in Manhattan and focus on where and when these accidents happens.

Where does it happens?

*This is an interactive map on which you can drag, zoom, and also search for a specific location.

From the heat map, it can be seen that the majority of car accidents on the entire Manhattan Island are concentrated in the central, southern, and northeastern regions. I have selected the six areas from these regions with the highest concentration of car accidents, which are Washington Heights, East Harlem, Upper East, Midtown South, Kips Bay, and Bowery-ChinaTown area.

By extracting Google Street View images from locations with high accident rates, it can be observed that these high-risk areas often share the following characteristics:

    A high volume of oversized vehicles
    Constructions along the roadside that can affect road width and visibility
    Traffic congestion
    A high number of parked cars on both sides of the road
    Incresing outdoor dining areas due to the pandemic block the road

Based on the monthly map, it can be seen that the car accident rate has been on the rise since the new year, while the lockdown at the end of March 2020 significantly reduced the car accident rate. However, with the lifting of the ban, the number of car accidents has gradually increased, but overall it has not rebounded to the level before the lockdown.

Through the daily map, it can be found that car accidents are more likely to occur during working hours, from 8am to 6pm, with slightly more accidents occurring in the afternoon than in the morning. Interestingly, the number of accidents at 1 pm shows a significant decrease compared to adjacent time periods, which may be due to the fact that most people take a lunch break at this time.
